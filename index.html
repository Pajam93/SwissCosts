Common questions

SwissCosts uses the official cantonal and communal tax multipliers to calculate cantonal, communal and federal income tax separately. Results are indicative and suitable for comparing locations. For certified tax advice, consult a licensed Treuhand or tax adviser.

Pillar 3a is Switzerland's voluntary private pension scheme. Contributions (up to CHF 7,258/year for employed people in 2025) are fully deductible from taxable income, which can meaningfully reduce your tax bill. SwissCosts shows the estimated saving when you enter a Pillar 3a amount.
Yes. B permit holders below a certain income threshold are taxed at source (Quellensteuer) rather than through a standard tax return. SwissCosts applies the correct calculation method based on the permit type you select.
Every Swiss commune sets its own tax multiplier (Steuerfuss) on top of the cantonal base rate. A low-tax commune like Wollerau (SZ) or Freienbach (SZ) can have an effective rate that's several percentage points lower than a high-tax neighbour in the same canton — potentially saving thousands per year.
